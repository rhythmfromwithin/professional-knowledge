# Adaptive Management of Microservices in Dynamic Computing Environments: A Taxonomy and Future Directions
> 原文: [https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25222](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.25222)

arXiv:2604.25222v1 Announce Type: new
Abstract: Microservice-based cloud applications face changing workloads, evolving request paths, variable network conditions, interference, and failures. These dynamics couple autoscaling, placement, routing, isolation, and remediation. The survey examines dynamics-aware adaptive management for microservices. Its taxonomy covers control locus, modeled dynamics, adaptation strategy, and evaluation evidence; objectives and telemetry are cross-cutting. A synthesis of 84 system entries and 13 evaluation artifacts shows that production dynamics are often partially modeled. Reported gains also depend on evaluation fidelity. Key future directions include cross-layer coordination, telemetry-to-control abstractions, safe learning-based control, and reproducible dynamic evaluation.
