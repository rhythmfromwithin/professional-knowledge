# InquiryBits: Sharing AI Conversation Traces to Support Collaboration Within Trust Boundaries
> 原文: [https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02763](https://arxiv.org/abs/2606.02763)

arXiv:2606.02763v1 Announce Type: new
Abstract: AI chat tools are shifting problem-solving and brainstorming conversations away from colleagues and into private AI interactions, reducing the shared awareness that supports team coordination. We introduce InquiryBits, a system that shares minimal summaries of AI conversations within configurable trust boundaries, separating AI-only analysis from human-visible sharing. In a study with 80 professionals, we find that people are broadly willing to share these traces to support collaboration and avoid duplicating work - but only within bounded groups. Comfort drops sharply as audience expands beyond close teams; the level of detail shared matters less than who can see it, with a preference for more detail over less within trusted groups. These findings suggest that trust boundaries, more than information granularity, may be the most impactful design parameter.
