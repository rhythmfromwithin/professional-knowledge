# Poisson Flow Model of Cortical Folding Pattern
> 原文: [https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17291](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.17291)

arXiv:2604.17291v1 Announce Type: new
Abstract: Cortical folding reflects coordinated neurodevelopmental processes and provides a sensitive marker of neurological disease. In juvenile myoclonic epilepsy (JME), structural abnormalities are subtle and spatially distributed, limiting the sensitivity of conventional morphometric measures such as cortical thickness. We introduce a Poisson flow model derived from gradients of the mean curvature field on the cortical surface. The method yields a smooth scalar field obtained from a Poisson equation, whose surface gradient defines a flow representation of folding organization. This representation enables spatially coherent characterization of sulcal--gyral patterns and provides a principled geometric framework for studying distributed cortical alterations in JME.
